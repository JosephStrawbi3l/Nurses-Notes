Common Nursing Abbreviations

Here are some commonly used abbreviations in the nursing practice:

Time & Frequency
  • PRN - As needed (Pro Re Nata)
  • NPO - Nothing by mouth (Nil Per Os)
  • STAT - Immediately
  • QD - Every day (q.d)
  • BID - Twice a day (b.i.d)
  • TID - Three times a day (t.i.d)
  • QID - Four times a day (q.i.d)
  • QOD - Every other day (q.o.d)
  • Q4H - Every 4 hours
  • AC - Before meals
  • PC - After meals
  • HS - At bedtime

Medication Routes
  • IV - Intravenous
  • IM - Intramuscular
  • ID - Intradermal
  • SC/SQ - Subcutaneous
  • PO - By mouth (Per Os)
  • SL - Sublingual
  • PR - Per rectum

Medication & Treatment
  • Rx - Prescription
  • Tx - Treatment
  • Dx - Diagnosis
  • IVF - Intravenous fluids
  • NS - Normal Saline

Assessment & Vitals
  • VS - Vital Signs
  • BP - Blood Pressure
  • HR - Heart Rate
  • RR - Respiratory Rate
  • T - Temperature
  • O2 Sat / SpO2 - Oxygen Saturation
  • LOC - Level of Consciousness
  • ROM - Range of Motion

Symptoms & Conditions
  • SOB - Shortness of Breath
  • N/V - Nausea and Vomiting
  • CP - Chest Pain
  • Fx - Fracture
