üßº Handwashing

As per the World Health Organization, handwashing is crucial in healthcare to prevent infections. Wash your hands before touching a patient to protect them from harmful germs, and again before any clean or medical procedures to avoid infection. Handwashing should be done after contact with body fluids, patients, or surfaces to protect both yourself and others.

How to Handwash?

  • Wet hands with water.
  • Apply enough soap.
  • Rub palms together.
  • Right palm over left dorsum and vice versa.
  • Palm to palm with fingers interlaced.
  • Backs of fingers to opposing palms.
  • Rub thumbs rotationally.
  • Rub fingertips in palms.
  • Rinse hands with water.
  • Dry with a single-use towel.
  • Use towel to turn off faucet.
  • Your hands are now clean.


ü©∫ Taking Vital Signs

Taking vital signs is essential in assessing a patient's current health status. It involves checking temperature, pulse, respiration, blood pressure, and oxygen saturation to detect any irregularities. Vital signs should be measured during initial assessment, before and after procedures, and when a patient's condition changes. These measurements help ensure timely care and protect both the patient and healthcare providers.

  • Temperature: Measure using oral, axillary, tympanic, or rectal route.
  • Pulse: Assess rate, rhythm, and strength.
  • Respiration: Observe breathing rate, depth, and rhythm.
  • Blood Pressure: Use a sphygmomanometer and stethoscope.
  • Oxygen Saturation: Use a pulse oximeter on the fingertip.

Legal & Ethical

Nursing is not just about skills and knowledge; it also involves acting in a responsible, ethical, and lawful manner. As a student nurse or practicing nurse, you must understand your role in upholding legal standards and applying ethical principles in all interactions. This section discusses the basic laws, rules, and ethical standards that guide professional behavior and decision-making in nursing practice.

‚öñÔ∏è Key Legal Terms & Concepts
  • INFORMED CONSENT
    Patients have the right to receive information about their condition, treatment options, and risks. Nurses often assist in obtaining informed consent by clarifying procedures and ensuring understanding.
  • CONFIDENTIALITY & DATA PRIVACY
    All patient information must be kept private. This includes their diagnosis, treatments, and personal information. In the Philippines, this is also supported by the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA 10173).
  • NEGLIGENCE vs. MALPRACTICE
    • Negligence: Failing to act as a reasonably prudent nurse would.
    • Malpractice: Professional negligence resulting in harm to the patient.
  • SCOPE OF PRACTICE
    This defines what tasks, roles, and responsibilities nurses are authorized to perform based on their license. Performing duties outside your scope is illegal.
  • PATIENT BILL OF RIGHTS
    Includes rights to safety, privacy, dignity, communication, and informed decision-making.
