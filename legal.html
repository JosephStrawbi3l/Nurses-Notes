Legal & Ethical


Nursing is not just about skills and knowledge; it also involves acting in a responsible, ethical, and lawful manner. As a student nurse or practicing nurse, you must understand your role in upholding legal standards and applying ethical principles in all interactions. This section discusses the basic laws, rules, and ethical standards that guide professional behavior and decision-making in nursing practice.

  • Informed Consent
    • Patients have the right to receive information about their condition, treatment options, and risks. Nurses often assist in obtaining informed consent by clarifying procedures and ensuring understanding.

  • Confidentiality & Data Privacy
    • All patient information must be kept private. This includes their diagnosis, treatments, and personal information. In the Philippines, this is also supported by the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA 10173).

  • Negligence vs. Malpractice
    • Negligence: Failing to act as a reasonably prudent nurse would.
    • Malpractice: Professional negligence resulting in harm to the patient.

  • Scope of Practice
    • This defines what tasks, roles, and responsibilities nurses are authorized to perform based on their license. Performing duties outside your scope is illegal.

  • Patient Bill of Rights
    • Includes rights to safety, privacy, dignity, communication, and informed decision-making.
