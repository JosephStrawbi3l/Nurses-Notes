Common Nursing Abbreviations

Here are some commonly used abbreviations in the nursing practice:

  1. ⏰ Time & Frequency
    • PRN - As needed (Pro Re Nata)
    • NPO - Nothing by mouth (Nil Per Os)
    • STAT - Immediately
    • QD - Every day (q.d)
    • BID - Twice a day (b.i.d)
    • TID - Three times a day (t.i.d)
    • QID - Four times a day (q.i.d)
    • QOD - Every other day (q.o.d)
    • Q4H - Every 4 hours
    • AC - Before meals
    • PC - After meals
    • HS - At bedtime
  2. 📍 Medication Routes
    • IV - Intravenous
    • IM - Intramuscular
    • ID - Intradermal
    • SC/SQ - Subcutaneous
    • PO - By mouth (Per Os)
    • SL - Sublingual
    • PR - Per rectum
  3. 💊 Medication & Treatment
    • Rx - Prescription
    • Tx - Treatment
    • Dx - Diagnosis
    • IVF - Intravenous fluids
    • NS - Normal Saline
  4. 📋 Assessment & Vitals
    • VS - Vital Signs
    • BP - Blood Pressure
    • HR - Heart Rate
    • RR - Respiratory Rate
    • T - Temperature
    • O2 Sat / SpO2 - Oxygen Saturation
    • LOC - Level of Consciousness
    • ROM - Range of Motion
  5. 🤒 Symptoms & Conditions
    • SOB - Shortness of Breath
    • N/V - Nausea and Vomiting
    • CP - Chest Pain
    • Fx - Fracture

Conversions & Computations

Accurate calculations are critical in nursing practice. They ensure that patients receive the correct dosage of medications, the right IV flow rate, and the proper timing of interventions. This section provides essential mathematical tools and common conversions used in bedside nursing, medication administration, and fluid management.


🔄 Basic Unit Conversions

  1. ⚖️ Weight:
    • 1 kilogram (kg) = 2.2 pounds (lbs)
    • 1 gram (g) = 1000 milligrams (mg)
  2. 🧪 Volume:
    • 1 teaspoon (tsp) = 5 milliliters (mL)
    • 1 tablespoon (tbsp) = 15 milliliters (mL)
    • 1 ounce (oz) = 30 milliliters (mL)
    • 1 cup = 240 mL
  3. 📏 Length:
    • 1 inch = 2.54 centimeters (cm)
  4. 🌡️ Temperature:
    • Celsius to Fahrenheit: °F = (°C × 9/5) + 32
    • Fahrenheit to Celsius: °C = (°F - 32) × 5/9

🧮 Basic Computations

  1. Medication Dosage Formula
    2.   (Desired dose ÷ Stock Dose) × Stock Volume = Volume to Administer
    💊 Example:
    • Doctor orders: 250mg
    • Available: 500mg tablet

    You give:
    (250÷500) × 1 tablet = 0.5 tablet

  2. IV Flow Rate Formula
    3.   Manual Drip Calculation (drops per minute):
      (Volume in mL × Drop factor) ÷ Time in minutes = gtt/min
    💧 Example:
    • Doctor orders: 500 mL over 4 hours, drop factor: 20 gtt/mL
    • Time: 4 hours = 240 minutes

    You regulate the flow rate to:
    (500 × 20)÷240=41.67≈42gtt/min


      Infusion Pump (mL per hour):
      Total Volume (mL) ÷ Total Time (hr) = mL/hr
    💧 Example:
    • Doctor orders: 1000 mL D5LR over 8 hours

    Then, the pump setting should be:
    1000 ÷ 8 = 125mL/hr


Other Useful Tips:

  1. Round answers carefully; follow institution rules (e.g., round to nearest tenth or whole number).
  2. Always double-check your math and consult references or a pharmacist if unsure.
  3. Keep a pocket calculator or use nursing apps for quick access.
  4. Master the metric system — it's the standard in healthcare.
