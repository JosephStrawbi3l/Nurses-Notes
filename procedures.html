Basic Nursing Procedures


Handwashing

As per the World Health Organization, handwashing is crucial in healthcare to prevent infections. You should wash your hands before touching a patient to protect them from harmful germs, and again before any clean or medical procedures to avoid introducing infection. It is also important to wash your hands immediately after exposure to body fluids to protect yourself and others. Additionally, handwashing should be done after touching a patient to prevent spreading germs, and after touching objects or surfaces around the patient, since germs can linger there as well. Following these key moments helps ensure the safety of both patients and healthcare workers.


How to Handwash?
  • Wet hands with water.
  • Apply enough soap.
  • Rub palms together.
  • Right palm over left dorsum and vice versa.
  • Palm to palm with fingers interlaced.
  • Backs of fingers to opposing palms.
  • Rub thumbs rotationally.
  • Rub fingertips in palms.
  • Rinse hands with water.
  • Dry with a single-use towel.
  • Use towel to turn off faucet.
  • Your hands are now clean.



Taking Vital Signs
  • Temperature: Measure using oral, axillary, tympanic, or rectal route.
  • Pulse: Assess rate, rhythm, and strength.
  • Respiration: Observe breathing rate, depth, and rhythm.
  • Blood Pressure: Use a sphygmomanometer and stethoscope.
  • Oxygen Saturation: Use a pulse oximeter on the fingertip.
